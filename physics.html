The OHM (Ω)

A conductor has a resistance of 1 ohm, if the current flowing through it is 1 ampere when the potential difference across it is 1 volt.

Ohm's Law

For some conductors (mainly metals), the resistance remains constant as the voltage and current changes. These are called ohmic conductors as they follow Ohm's Law.

Ohm's Law states that, for certain conductors at constant temperature, the current is directly proportional to the voltage.
Derivation:
∵ I ∝ V
∴ IR = V → V = IR

The Ohmmeter

An ohmmeter is used to measure resistance. It is connected across the conductor it is measuring R for.

Temperature and Resistance

Metallic Conductor

Resistance increases linearly with temperature.

Metals are good conductors as they have many closely packed atoms. However, when they are heated, many electrons become thermally excited and are released and they begin to collide with each other.

Thermistor

A semiconductor whose resistance decreases rapidly as temperature increases.

Thermistors have minimal amounts of electrons available for conduction. It is an insulator at low temperatures.

Variable Resistors
  • Fixed/practical resistors have a set resistance which can't be changed.
  • Variable resistors can be adjusted, e.g. rheostat.

Capacitance

A capacitor is a device capable of storing an electrical charge. It will hold the electrical charge until required.

Capacitance is the amount of charge a capacitor is capable of holding.

Formula: Q = CV
Q = charge, C = capacitor, V = voltage.
Unit = farad (F)
Example Question:
What is the charge on a 600 pF capacitor when it has a potential difference of 2KV?

Solution:
Q = (600 x 10-12) (2 x 103) = 1.2 x 10-6 C

Changing Capacitance

  • Bring an oppositely charged charge near a conductor. The conductor will attract the charge... This increases capacitance.
  • Bring a similarly charged charge near a conductor... This decreases capacitance.

Electrolytic Capacitor

  • An electrolytic capacitor has two plates separated by a dielectric material and is rolled up.
  • A dielectric is a non-conductive material, e.g. paper, ceramic.

Parallel Plate Capacitor

These have two plates separated by a dielectric material.

Application of Capacitors

A capacitor stores charge and can act as temporary battery. It can release its charge on demand.

Example: Camera Flash
You press a button to charge up the capacitor. A light comes on when it is fully charged. You press the button to take the picture and the capacitor discharges, releasing all of its charge.
